Symbols that should be retired or renamed

This exercise also exposed a few naming problems that should be fixed before QCFT becomes more mature.

1. H is overloaded

Currently it means both:

the Hubble parameter ,

and the internal curvature tensor .


That's not acceptable in a canonical theory. One of them should be renamed. I would lean toward keeping  for cosmology (because it's standard) and renaming the internal curvature tensor to something unique, perhaps , , or another symbol that reflects its geometric role.

2. Q is potentially overloaded

We've been using  as the topological winding number, while also talking about electric charge. If electric charge is an emergent property of winding, that's fine—but we should reserve  for the winding invariant during development to avoid confusion until the derivation is complete.

3. ρ needs discipline

Throughout QCFT, ρ must always mean η density.

Never matter density.

If we need baryonic density, use a different symbol (for example, ), matching standard cosmological notation.
